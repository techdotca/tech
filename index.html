Change your DNS for faster internet

MAY 2020

DNS stands for Domain Name System. It basically translates web addresses into IP addresses. For example, when typing facebook.com, it will translate it into an IP address so that our device can connect. DNS services are normally provided by your Internet Service Provider(ISP). But major companies also offer their own DNS services.

Your router will set the default DNS to the one provided by your ISP. However, your internet traffic will be seen by your ISP and they could sell your data. There are DNS services provided for free by some big companies such as Google. Google's DNS settings are 8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4. However, there may be privacy concerns with Google. Furthermore, as Google's DNS is used by many users, the DNS resolving take time and make your internet slower.

Currently, the fastest and safest DNS settings are provided from Cloudflare. Cloudflare is one of the biggest web-infrastructure and website-security company. Cloudflare does not log your information. They even go an extra mile by regularly doing audits to prove that they do not log or sell any information. Their DNS settings are 1.1.1.1 and 1.0.0.1. They recently released additional DNS settings that blocked malware and adult content. For malware blocking only, 1.1.1.2 and 1.0.0.2. For malware and adult content blocking, 1.1.1.3 and 1.0.0.3.

DNS settings can be changed on any device by going into Wi-Fi settings. The best solution would be to change the DNS in the router settings, thus applying it network-wide.
