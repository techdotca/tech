Change your DNS for faster internet

MAY 2020

DNS stands for Domain Name System. It basically translates web addresses into IP addresses. For example, when typing facebook.com, it will translate it into an IP address so that our device can connect. DNS services are normally provided by your Internet Service Provider(ISP). But major companies also offer their own DNS services.
Your router will set the default DNS to the one from your ISP. However, your internet traffic will be seen by your ISP and they could sell your data. There are DNS services provided for free by some big companies such as Google. Google's DNS settings are 8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4.
